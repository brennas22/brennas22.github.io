Generate Marketing

Generate Marketing

Generate is Northeastern's Student-Led Product Development Studio. We launched in the spring of 2016 and began working on student ventures, helping them with everything from prototyping to concept development.

My Role

I designed the logo and brand guidelines for all marketing materials. I also created a number of posters, handouts and business cards.

My first task as the designer for Generate was to create the logo. Initially, we discussed the imagery of a circuit, lightning bolt or piston. We also selected a color palette to use as a starting point; we selected dulled colors to convey a more serious tone, however as you can see in the process book we eventually opted for a brighter set to attract more attention and draw focus to our logo.

We also experimented heavily with font, looking at a variety of choices that had an "engineering" or technical feel to them. The main factor in our choice of font was the shape and styling of the "g" in both uppercase and lowercase. We ultimately selected Circular Std as our brand's font due to the smaller tick at the top of the lowercase g.

In our second iteration, mildly inspired by Instagram's redesign earlier in 2016, we decided to opt for a more symmetrical feel in our logo, changing the shape of the blades to be uniform. We also tweaked our color palette slightly in order to be more cohesive across our brand.

For the brand guidelines I consulted with Generate's Marketing Director, Nick Parente. We designed a guide encompassing rules for logos, colors, font, patterns, etc. as well as the social media guidelines. The entire guide can be viewed here

Another important aspect of Generate's branding was its business cards. We chose to design 2 distinct, yet cohesive business cards: One for the management team with their specific contact information, and one for Generate as a whole. The management team cards (right) are more straight foward and serious as they may be handed out to potential clients, industry mentors, or donors. The general cards (left) have a more spunky design on the back and convey a more fun vibe to the organization as they are more likely going to be distributed to students on campus in order to encourage them to join the team.
