Pop Up Desk

Pop Up Desk

This pop up desk was the final prototype that I worked on in a team of three as part of a class called Product Design Methodology throughout the fall semester of 2017.

Features

The pop up desk is meant to be a portable, easy and intuitive to use, and lightweight desk for students on the go.

It is highly modular and, though not demonstrated in the protoype, many additional features, such as a light, a USB bank, and more could be easily added. The prototype does demonstrate a slide out mouse pad, a slide out cupholder and additional space (perhaps for a cellphone), a pencil case space and a general workspace. The height is also adjustable, as well as the angle of the desk.

Process Book

Below is a closer look at the important steps of the product design process we followed in order to create this prototype

Problem Statement

How might we create an efficient, productive and portable space that is adaptable for users between the ages 14 to 22 years old to do schoolwork on and off campus?

Competitive Product Research / IP Search

Product: 3-in-1 Product: A desk, chair, bag (Convinenture)

Stengths: Increased Productivity, Portable and Lightweight, Compact, Multipurpose kit, Carters to various lifestyle, Water Resistant SB EVA Material, Slots for cord management, Laptop ventilation holes

Weaknesses: Size limitations: 17 in, Only support under 230 lbs, Not a reasonable price: $193, Not stylish, No Portable charging system

Product: Multifunctional Bamboo Laptop Desk

Stengths: Adjustable angle, Additional storage available, Sturdy material, Sits above lap instead of directly on, Has a cup holder, Decent price (33$), Has fans to cool laptop

Weaknesses: Heavy - 5.5 lbs, Clunky, Have to bring your own seating, Poorly constructed according to reviews, Legs are difficult to maneuver, Height is not adjustable

Product: Folding adjustable lap desk with built-in 10,000mAh powerbank and LED light

Stengths: Built-in 10,000mAh battery, Has an LED light, Can carry up to 25 pounds, Fits 17 inch laptops, Foldable legs,Can rest tablets and phones as well

Weaknesses: Somewhat heavy at 4.8 pounds, Not very stable with flimsy legs, No locks for leg height position, Nothing to hold phone in place, Nothing stops tablets from falling over if the tablet is too vertical on the shelf

Brainstorm and Sketching

Decision Matrix

After sketching, our next step was to create a decision matrix to narrow down the options

We rated our requirements based on importances as either a 1, 3, or 7 and then did the same with how well each solution option fulfils the criteria. The scores are then multiplied by the weights and added to determine which one was the best fit.

This was the midway point of the class, at which point we presented our work so far, ending in a Gantt Chart of our future work

Makestorm

Next we used cardboard (and a lot of tape!) to prototype our "pop up top" idea, which won our decision matrix.

Final Prototype

For our final protoype we opted to use balsa wood due to its lightweight nature, low cost, and high strength to weight ratio. We chose laser cutting as our manufacturing method because it is easily and accurately repeatable, reliable, and cheap (free in our case!)

Above: sketches and Adobe Illustrator files for the laser cut of the final prototype. Below: Photographs of the finished piece and assembly process.

Key Takeaways

This project an interesting challenge for me because of the engineering focus. I learned a lot about the overall process of product development as well as laser cutting and a little bit of mechanical engineering. I also was able to work on my presentation and teamwork skills.
