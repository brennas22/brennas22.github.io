About me!

Hello again.

Here's the basics: my name is Brenna, my pronouns are she/hers, and I'm a designer! I grew up in the Boston area and now live in Central Square with two roommates and my cat, Atticus. I love to cook and read, and my favorite snack is dates with peanut butter. I believe that a hot dog is not a sandwich.

Relevant information.

I graduated magna cum laude from Northeastern University in Boston, MA in 2019 with a degree in integrated software and interaction design (it was an independent major, hence the longer name). My courses were a combination of design, computer science, and healthcare related electives, due to my strong interest in accessibility. I graduated with University Honors Program Distinction, Honors in the College of Arts, Media, and Design, and as a member of Northeastern's Huntington 100.

I have experience in strategy and design research as well as pixel pushing and front end development (I built this website!). I also have worked on copy writing, email marketing, and Shopify (both site design and running business operations on the platform). I have expertise in accessibility and a passion for integrating it as a consideration throughout the design process. This website complies with all ADA 508 standards for contrast and is keyboard & screenreader navigable.

I'm currently designing at the Innovation & Digital Health Accelerator at Boston Children's Hospital.

Irrelevant information.

Outside of work, I spend a lot of time cooking and baking and purchasing too many condiments that all have to be refrigerated. I chronicle my food adventures on Instagram and I love making homemade tortellini and sourdough bread. I deeply enjoy reading and am currently absorbing everything I can regarding agriculture policy in the United States. I have a Harry Potter tattoo and a missing finger.
