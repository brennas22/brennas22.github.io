unCommon Foods

unCommon Foods

In Experience Design I we were tasked with creating an intervention in the Emerald Necklace - the series of Olmsted parks in Boston. I designed a new food experience for the Boston common that would emphasize local sustainable food.

I developed a rough business plan for funding the experience, as well as the physical structures that would be placed in the commons, and an app to facilitate navigation of the new features.

this video is a short synopsis of the project

This project began with an ethnographic study in and around Boston's public parks (The Emerald Necklace). I traveled to the Boston Common; in my first visit I only observed patrons in a natural habitat so as not to corrupt my findings. I observed a number of fast food stands and restaurants surrounding the park and noted that people either purchased that food or brought their own and enjoyed a meal picnic style.

These observations led me to formulate a number of open ended questions to ask patrons during my next visit. I asked about their time spent in the common (frequency, activity, etc.) and then asked them to tell me their feelings about the food scene in the area. Ultimately I decided to pursue the topic of healthy, local food in the commons as the core concept for my semester long project.

Below is the poster presented as part of my final presentation, followed by an explanation.

The solution that I created has 2 main facets: The first is a business plan that sustains local up and coming chefs. The way it works is that investors give money to maintain the kiosks in the park as well as funding to the chefs. The chefs in the program are not already established in the Boston food scene, but can apply and be selected for the program. The funding from the investors allows the chefs to put together a special menu to be served in the common for a limited time. Customers can then order the food via app (below) and provide feedback. This feedback helps the chefs improve, but also helps the investors know which chefs have the best potential to be successful in a full scale restaurant or food truck. The quick-changing cycle of chefs in the kiosks allows a vast array of new chefs to showcase their talents and potentially find an investor, which helps the Boston food scene grow, pushing out the fast food in the area and bringing a more local and authentic feel to the Boston Common, which is often known as the quintessential Boston thing to see for tourists.

App

I did an initial mock up of the app that would be used for ordering food in the park in November 2016 using Adobe Experience Design (images can be found at the bottom of the page). I recently (February 2017) decided that my work could be improved. Using Sketch and learning Principle for the first time, I redesigned the app and added animations and special transitions.
