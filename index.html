Who am I?

My name is Brenna Sorkin and I'm a UX designer at Boston Children's Hospital's Innovation and Digital Health Accelerator. I graduated from Northeastern University in May 2019 with a degree in computer science and UX design, with a focus in healthcare and accessibility. I'm extremely passionate about healthcare, in particular patient experience, and I pride myself on making my designs visually and technically accessible. You can learn more about me here.

What am I good at?

I'm a full stack experience designer (I just made that term up, but I think it works!) I have expertise in research, information architecture, interaction design, and service design - to name a few. I also built this website, which is compliant with all accessibility recommendations for color contrast, as well as keyboard and screen reader navigable (only Google Chrome - working on expanding!)
